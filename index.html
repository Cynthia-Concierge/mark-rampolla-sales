The $200M Exit That Changed Everything

In 2013, I sold ZICO Coconut Water to Coca-Cola for $200 million. By every external measure, I'd made it.

Inside? I was completely empty.

The operating system I'd used to build ZICO — the constant hustle, the never-enough mentality, the optimization of every external metric — had worked for the business. But it had broken me.

So I spent 7 years rebuilding from the inside out. I developed a framework for optimizing the internal operating system before optimizing the external business.

Then I bought ZICO back from Coca-Cola. And scaled it beyond its previous peak — but this time, without losing myself in the process.

Now I run GroundForce Capital (managing $600M across brands like Liquid Death, Bobbie, and Righteous Felon) and coach founders on the same framework that saved me.

The Freedom First Framework

Optimize Your Internal OS First

Most founders optimize revenue, team, operations — everything external. But if your internal operating system is running on burnout and scarcity, everything you build on top of it is unstable.

Nervous System Regulation

The most underrated competitive advantage in business. Learn to operate from a regulated nervous system instead of constant fight-or-flight. Better decisions, sustainable performance, actual presence.

Build From Abundance, Not Scarcity

The hustle-culture operating system is rooted in "not enough." The Freedom First framework teaches you to build from abundance — and counterintuitively, that's when the business accelerates.

Make the Internal and External Match

When your internal state matches your external goals, everything compounds. When they're misaligned, you're fighting yourself every step of the way. This framework aligns them.
